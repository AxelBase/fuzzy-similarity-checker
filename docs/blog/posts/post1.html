What is Fuzzy String Matching?

Published: December 1, 2025

Fuzzy string matching is the ability to compare two pieces of text and determine how similar they are, even when the texts are not exactly identical. Unlike traditional exact matching, which returns either a perfect match or no match at all, fuzzy matching returns a similarity score between 0% and 100%.

This is essential in real-world scenarios where humans introduce typos, abbreviations, different word order, or slight rephrasing. For example, "Jon Smith" and "John Smyth" should be considered highly similar, even though they are spelled differently. The same applies to addresses, product names, user comments, or any free-form text.

Why Exact Matching Isn’t Enough

Exact matching works perfectly for passwords or IDs, but fails completely with natural language. A user searching for “best noise cancelling headphones” might type “best noise canceling head phones” and still expect relevant results. Content platforms need to detect reposted articles even when sentences are slightly reworded. Schools want to flag suspected plagiarism when students paraphrase sources.

Fuzzy matching solves all of these problems by understanding that small differences do not always mean different meaning.

Common Real-World Use Cases

  • Detecting duplicate or near-duplicate support tickets
  • Blocking prohibited phrases in chat while allowing natural variations
  • Finding similar customer feedback across thousands of reviews
  • Matching messy data during imports or migrations
  • Improving search relevance on forums and knowledge bases
