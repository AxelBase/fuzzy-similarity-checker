Private Blocklist Checking with Client-Side Fuzzy Matching

Published: December 5, 2025

Many platforms maintain internal blocklists of banned usernames, slurs, drug terms, or brand impersonations. Sending every user message to a server for checking creates serious privacy and legal risks. Client-side fuzzy checking solves this elegantly.

You load the sensitive blocklist once (locally, encrypted if needed), then every new message is compared entirely in the browser. Nothing leaves the user’s device.

Common Scenarios

  • Preventing new account creation with variations of banned usernames
  • Blocking coded drug sales language (“710 friendly”, “420”, etc.)
  • Stopping brand impersonation (“PayPal-Support”, “Amazon Help”)
  • Filtering harassment that uses spaces or symbols to bypass filters

Partial Ratio shines here because banned terms are usually short and appear inside longer text. A score above 90% is almost always a hit, even with deliberate obfuscation.

Because everything happens in the browser, compliance teams avoid storing or logging user content while still maintaining strong protection. This approach is now used by several privacy-focused chat apps and forums.

Privacy and safety are not mutually exclusive when the right tools are used correctly.
