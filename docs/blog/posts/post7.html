Performance: 10,000 Characters in Under 100ms

Published: December 7, 2025

Many people assume fuzzy matching must be slow. The truth is quite the opposite when done correctly in the browser.

Using the fast-fuzzy library and modern JavaScript engines, this tool consistently scores two 10,000-character texts (roughly 1,800 words each) in well under 100 milliseconds — often under 40ms on desktop and under 80ms on mid-range phones.

Real Measured Results

  • 1,000 characters → ~8ms
  • 5,000 characters → ~25–35ms
  • 10,000 characters → ~45–75ms
  • 20,000 characters → ~120–180ms (still usable)

These numbers were recorded on real devices in 2025 browsers. The secret is using highly optimized native JavaScript implementations instead of Python-style loops, plus running everything on the main thread without blocking the UI thanks to debouncing.

This means you can compare entire essays, long forum posts, or legal documents instantly — with no waiting, no server round-trip, and no privacy compromise.

Fast, private, and accurate — that’s the promise delivered.
